# Reading MCP Descriptor Poisoning: The Supply-Chain Actor's Move

**Status: [OPEN] hypothesis. Stub, next in the series.**

Part 2 of the Persistence Typology series. Follows the model set by `01-memory-poisoning.md`: the move nobody logs as a choice, what the mechanism is (with the framework mapping), the actor read, the uniquely counter-adversary read, what it leaves behind, what it does not tell you, and where it sits in the typology.

The claim this piece will develop: poisoning a tool descriptor (AI Agent Tool Poisoning, AML.T0110, added to ATLAS in the October 2025 MITRE and Zenity Labs collaboration, triggered through AI Agent Tool Invocation, AML.T0053) reveals a one-to-many, supply-chain mindset. Where memory poisoning is the opportunist's single-target foothold, descriptor poisoning is the actor thinking about a population of victims: organized-eCrime or nation-state supply-chain tradecraft, not opportunism. The registry is the watering hole, and the actor who poisons it has already stopped thinking about any one target.
